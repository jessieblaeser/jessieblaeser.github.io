Scraping Federal Court Nominees

I built this tool to pull information from the Senate Judiciary Committee website. The code navigates from the committee's homepage to it's past nomination meetings, and dowloads and reads each nominee's S form (a questionnaire). Using regular expressions, exported the following information to a CSV:

  • Name
  • Nominated position
  • Law school
  • Pro bono work experience, which served as my unit of analysis

Using textual analysis, I created categories for the type of (required) pro bono work completed by those appointed to the federal courts. The final output of this project is a GeoJSON choropleth map that displays pro bono work categories by district.

Abandoned but not forgotten: New York’s once-booming oil industry and the wells it left behind

New York's oil industry peaked in the late 1800s, when it produced just under 7 million barrels of oil in a single year. My team and I analyzed data from the Department of Environmental Conservation to find that the former seat of the oil industry in New York State, Allegany County, now has more abandoned and unplugged oil wells than active ones. Statewide, the DEC has over 4,700 wells still in need of plugging. In the meantime, each abandoned and unplugged well poses an environmental threat.

Ticket to Ride: Europe Analysis

What is Ticket to Ride, you say? Imagine Settlers of Catan, only with more public transit and less sheep. In this project, I visualize the most likely routes to be taken by players during the boardgame, Ticket to Ride: Europe.

By applying probability measures and Dijkstra's algorithm in python (all inspired and assisted by an analysis of the original Ticket to Ride: USA board)), I created heatmap visualizations using matplotlib that help demonstrate different strategies a player might take in order to win. All aboard!
